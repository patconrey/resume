About Me

I'm a student at the University of Kentucky. I'm in my fourth year pursuing two degrees in Electrical Engineering and Mathematics. I've been designing since I was 15 or so and I only recently got into programming (see below). Math is my favorite thing in the world with a particular bias towards analysis and topology.

I mainly focus on iOS development with Swift, though I'm more than familiar with PHP, Node, and front end web languages like HTML, SCSS, and JavaScript. In fact, I wrote this website from scratch and it uses an elementary mix of SCSS for styling, Node for serving, semantic and accessible HTML, jQuery because #jQuery, and Bootstrap for its grid.

Mosy

Mosy was a blast to program. It started with a personal challenge: how fast can I develop a robust chat application? Using Firebase for authentication and data storage was huge help. The design was largely inspired by Google's Material paradigm.
