Mosy

Mosy was a blast to program. It started with a personal challenge: how fast can I develop a robust chat application? Using Firebase for authentication and data storage was a huge help, and OneSignal beautifully handled push notifications. The design was largely inspired by Google's Material paradigm.

This app is fairly straightforward. Users can create an account with their email and a password with some other information, e.g., profile picture, birthday, etc. Users can search for others and start conversations, lock certain conversations so somebody would need a password to view the conversation, send images, videos, update their password and email, save videos and images and later view them in their gallery through custom media players.

All of the previously described features are built, but there's still work to do before I put it on the App Store. I'd like to run a few UX tests on the account creation process and make some narrative based onboarding screens.

Juke

Juke was a fun, small project I made for a friend. She didn't listen to music too much since she liked CDs and she was rarely in her car. I got the idea for a music player interface that prominently displays a spinning CD of the album art for the song that's playing.

This app isn't connected to the internet in any way and just came with a preloaded playlist of songs. The code isn't hosted on my GitHub, but if you're interested please feel free to reach out!

The songs in the mockup are The Revivalists' "Soul Fight" and Travis Scott's "coordinate," gratefully used here without intent of copyright infringement.
